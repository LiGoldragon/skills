# Skill — prose

## Rules

Make the writing carry one pressure. Delete every sentence that does not sharpen
that pressure, change the reader's options, or preserve necessary evidence.

Prefer concrete nouns and active verbs. Let the chosen words do the argument;
do not explain that the argument is important.

Use present tense for standing guidance and current interpretation. Use past
tense for observed events. Avoid roadmap tense unless the sentence assigns an
actual next action.

Open at the load-bearing point. Do not warm up with throat-clearing, status,
method narration, or a promise of what the piece will do.

Define a technical term once, at first use, only if the reader needs the gloss to
act. After that, use the term without apology.

Keep sources subordinate to the prose. Quote only the phrase that carries weight;
frame it with the decision it supports. Do not build a shrine around a quote.

Prefer omission to explanation. Trust the strongest image, fact, or rule to
stand. If a paragraph needs a sentence explaining why it matters, rewrite the
paragraph around the mattering thing.

Use rhythm deliberately: short sentences for verdicts, longer sentences for
relations, lists for parallel obligations, tables only for comparisons.

Do not hedge with empty safety words: generally, simply, notably, arguably,
essentially, basically, in many ways. Keep uncertainty only when it changes the
reader's action.

Delete meta-prose: "this section explains", "it is worth noting", "as mentioned
above", "the key takeaway". State the thing.

Delete negative-contrast filler: "not merely X but Y", "more than just", "not
only". Name the positive claim unless the contrast prevents a real mistake.

Do not over-capitalize concepts to manufacture importance. Capitalize names,
not vibes.

When the prose feels false, inspect the claim before polishing the sentence.
Weak writing often signals a weak distinction, missing evidence, or borrowed
voice.

When a piece changes purpose, change its form. A rule, report, essay, comment,
commit message, and user reply do different work; do not force one cadence onto
all of them.
