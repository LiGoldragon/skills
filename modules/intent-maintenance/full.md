# Skill — intent maintenance

## Rules

Use intent maintenance to keep the Spirit intent log precise: fold mistaken standalone clarifications into targets, supersede contradicted records, retire obsolete records, and remove entries only with psyche authorization.

Start by reading the relevant intent neighborhood. Classify each candidate as new intent, clarification, supersession, retirement, removal, manifestation gap, matter, or non-intent.

Do not collapse conflicts by taste. Preserve the conflict or ask the psyche for an explicit decision.

## Clarifications

If a record only clarifies another record, attach the clarification to the target through the deployed maintenance operation. The standalone clarification should not remain as a competing active record.

A clarification is valid only when the psyche's words identify or clearly answer the target. If the target is ambiguous, ask.

## Supersession and retirement

When the psyche overrides earlier intent, use the typed supersession or retirement surface. Name what is superseded and keep the replacement meaning traceable to verbatim psyche words.

Do not treat ordinary age as supersession. A record is stale only when later accepted intent, current reality, or explicit psyche retirement makes it stale.

## Removal

Remove only when the psyche authorizes removal or the maintenance surface explicitly models the cleanup being requested. Record enough justification to make the removal auditable without preserving private substance in public.

Prefer lowered certainty, retirement, or supersession when the record remains useful history.

## Manifestation gaps

If the log is correct but the owning guidance is stale, edit the guidance. Do not create a new intent record to compensate for an unmanifested old one.

After maintenance, verify that duplicate standalone clarifications are gone, superseded records name their successor, and affected guidance no longer contradicts accepted intent.
